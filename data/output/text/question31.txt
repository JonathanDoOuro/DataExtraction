 
 
Gaslighting  is a form of 
psychological abuse where 
a person or group makes 
someone question their 
sanity, perception of reality, or memories. People experiencing gaslighting 
often feel confused, anxious, and unable to trust themselves. The term gaslighting derives from the 1938 
play and 1944 film  “Gaslight” , in which a husband 
manipulates his wife into thinking she has a mental illness by dimming their gas -fueled lights and telling her she is 
hallucinating. While anyone can experience gaslighting, it is especially common in intimate relationships and in social 
interactions where there is an imbalance of power. A person who is on the receiving end of this behavior is 
experiencing abuse.
